Noah Yi-Ting Hung

PhD Candidate · Machine Learning & Data Science · Georgia State University

I am a PhD candidate in Machine Learning and Data Science, Department of Mathematics and Statistics at Georgia State University, under the guidance of Professor Li-Hsiang Lin. My research focuses on the intersection of machine learning, image science, and uncertainty control, with a particular interest in non-parametric modeling and portfolio optimization.

I received my Master's in Joint Statistics and Computer Science at Purdue University, where I worked with Professor Qi Guo on computer vision. Prior to my PhD, I was a graduate research assistant under Professor Henry Horng-Shing Lu, working on deep mixture density networks. I completed undergraduate studies at National Taiwan University with Ku-Wei Liao on reliability research.

Selected Publications

AURA: Adaptive Uncertainty-guided Refinement and Annotation for LLM-as-a-Judge

Noah Yi-Ting Hung, et al.  (Preprint)

arXiv 2025 LLM Evaluation

We propose AURA, a framework that iteratively refines LLM-judge consistency via a trainable encoder, OT-based relabeling, and human-guided verification. The system combines confidence updates from model evidence, local consistency, anchor support, and transport inflow to produce reliable pairwise preference labels with minimal human annotation cost.

Auditing LLM Preference Alignment via Positive-Unlabeled Optimal Transport

Noah Yi-Ting Hung, et al.  (Preprint)

arXiv 2025 RLHF

We introduce a framework for auditing LLM preference alignment using Positive-Unlabeled (PU) learning and optimal transport. Reward model embeddings are constructed from Chatbot Arena comparisons, then difference embeddings are transported between a labeled positive pool and an unlabeled pool to identify misaligned preference annotations.

PDF arXiv BibTeX

Sparse Deep Additive Model with Interactions: Enhancing Interpretability and Predictability

Noah Yi-Ting Hung, Li-Hsiang Lin, Vince D. Calhoun  (Submitted)

arXiv 2025 Interpretable ML

SDAMI combines sparsity-driven feature selection with deep subnetworks for flexible function approximation. Unlike conventional black-box deep learning models, SDAMI explicitly disentangles main effects and interaction effects to enhance interpretability while maintaining predictive power on small or moderate samples with high-dimensional features.

PDF arXiv BibTeX

Deep P-Spline: Theory, Fast Tuning, and Application

Noah Yi-Ting Hung, Li-Hsiang Lin, Vince D. Calhoun  (Under Revision)

arXiv 2025 SPES Award Winner

We introduce a difference penalty that automates knot selection and simplifies neuron selection in deep neural networks. Deep P-Spline (DPS) extends conventional DNN modeling and forms the basis for a latent variable framework using the Expectation-Conditional Maximization (ECM) algorithm for efficient structure tuning with theoretical guarantees.

PDF arXiv BibTeX

Semi-Markov Process-Driven Maintenance Scheduling for Tainter Gate System Considering Multiple Limit States

John Thedy, Kuo-Wei Liao, Noah Yi-Ting Hung  (Accepted)

JSHM 2024 Reliability

Adopting the Semi-Markov Process, which accommodates non-exponential distributions of state durations, to formulate optimal maintenance strategies for Tainter gate systems noted for prolonged dormancy and significant operational uncertainties.
